Our Approach to Education

It is our distinctive pedagogical approach that sets our school apart from others. Our methodology represents an innovative endeavour to amalgamate the finest educational concepts from both Indian and global practices. JSIS an integration of the philosophies of Mahatma Gandhi, Rabindranath Tagore and Madam Maria Montessori. Self-sufficiency, humility, co-existence, empathy, courage and the ability to recognise your true potential and spirit; are our primary goals. We embrace a learning-by-doing philosophy where knowledge intertwines seamlessly with practical activities, fostering the development of enduring moral character in our students. At the heart of our educational philosophy lies universal peace and tolerance, fundamental tenets that underpin our commitment to nurturing humility in every child, thus contributing to a future world characterized by harmony and goodwill.

"Every Child is Unique" is not merely a statement but a guiding principle that propels us to unlock the inherent potential within each student, both academically and beyond. Our objective is to inspire every child to envision grand aspirations and carve a distinctive path for themselves. Through personalized attention and encouragement, we strive to transform each child's identity, turning their mere signatures into enduring autographs of success.

The JSIS is supported by a seasoned Education and Resource Council comprising experienced professionals dedicated to ensuring academic excellence. Through rigorous academic audits, ongoing teacher training programs, and consistent monitoring and support, we uphold a commitment to delivering quality education.

Our happy classrooms serve as our hallmark, permeating every aspect of our curriculum delivery. We champion a curriculum delivery model that prioritizes joy, creativity, and stress-free learning. Yoga and theatre take centre stage alongside sports, with a strong emphasis on environmental education and a blend of Indian values and global cultures.

At JSIS, our culture is deeply rooted in our origin, vision, and mission, fostering a rich working environment characterized by strong team spirit, open communication, transparency, and a child- and environment-friendly attitude. We go beyond preparing children for their careers; we prepare them for life as global citizens.

The process of Education

"Experience is the only true teacher; all else is just information." - Albert Einstein

"Wisdom is gained not through books alone, but through the journey of life itself." - Confucius Leading educationists worldwide emphasize that learning gains more significance when the emphasis is on the processes of learning rather than solely on content. If the ultimate goal of education is to foster individual understanding, then teaching methods must accommodate the diversity among children. To uphold these guiding principles, we have implemented the following approaches at JSIS...

Engaging Learning Methodology (ELM)

JSIS's Engaging Learning Programme encourages children to actively engage in their learning journey. Educators are prompted to question fundamental assumptions such as who the primary learner is, whose responsibility it is to learn, whether learning merely involves passive listening, performing tasks, and asking questions, or if it encompasses higher-order thinking like analysis, synthesis, and evaluation, and if it promotes critical thinking by utilizing logical assessment against historical data and real-world scenarios. In an ELM environment, children are not passive recipients of knowledge but are instead encouraged to question, discuss, reflect, and explore on their own. Teachers play a facilitating role rather than dictating and controlling how and what a child learns.

Experiential thematic learning

Experiential thematic learning program encourages associative thinking and holistic learning. Thematic elements such as the sky, air, fire, water, and earth serve as foundational pillars in theme-based learning, intertwining various disciplines such as science, social studies, and environmental studies into a unified educational experience. This approach diverges from traditional compartmentalized learning methods, where subjects are taught in isolation, by fostering an interconnected understanding of diverse concepts. In theme-based learning, each thematic element acts as a focal point around which related topics and subjects are explored, allowing students to delve deeper into the intricacies of the natural world and human society. For example, when studying the theme of water, students may not only learn about its chemical properties in science class but also its significance in geography, history, and culture. By connecting these interdisciplinary concepts within a thematic framework, students develop a holistic perspective that encourages critical thinking, problem-solving, and a deeper appreciation for the interconnectedness of knowledge across various domains. Thus, theme-based learning not only enhances academic comprehension but also cultivates a broader understanding of the world and its complexities. This approach provides opportunities for hands-on exploration, experimentation, and creativity, fostering critical thinking and facilitating a smooth transition from familiar concepts to new ones.

Learning Styles

Every individual learns and processes information differently, with each person having a preferred learning style. Identifying and understanding one's learning style enhances learning effectiveness. Awareness of diverse learning styles enables teachers to tailor lessons to accommodate various learning preferences.

Mind Map Tools

The mind map is a potent technique that unlocks the brain's potential. It serves as an ideal tool for tapping into natural creativity and leveraging it for effective problem-solving. Mind maps aid in maintaining balance between the right and left brain hemispheres, focusing thoughts in a relaxed yet concentrated manner through the incorporation of colours and images alongside logic and lines. Mind mapping is useful for clarifying thoughts, organizing information, facilitating clear communication, and absorbing new information.

Circle Time

Circle time fosters group interaction aimed at self-awareness and mutual understanding. It provides a platform for children to develop life skills such as effective communication, emotional intelligence, anger management, peer mediation, and conflict resolution. Additionally, circle time facilitates perspective building, group discussions, and consensus building, promoting respectful and sensitive interaction among children.

Holistic Thinking

JSIS encourages holistic thinking by exposing children to perspectives that transcend reductionist approaches and short-term outcomes. Through questioning, debating, and collaborative problem-solving, children are encouraged to consider experiences from various angles, fostering a broader understanding of issues. Collaborative work and problem-solving exercises are particularly engaging for children..

Multiple Intelligence Approach

Howard Gardner's theory of multiple intelligences recognizes a diverse range of human capabilities, challenging the notion of intelligence as a singular entity. By acknowledging different intelligences, educators can adopt varied teaching approaches to accommodate diverse learning styles, enhancing the likelihood of effective learning and minimizing distortion.
