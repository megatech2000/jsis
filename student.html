Student Conduct Policy

The student conduct policy at JSIS ensures the maintenance of an atmosphere and ethos that fosters learning, rooted in respect for each individual. Positive behavior serves as the cornerstone of our learning-centred approach. Every student is entitled to learn in an environment that is safe, conducive to learning, and devoid of disruptions. Similarly, every staff member has the right to work in an environment that empowers them to provide a positive learning experience for all students. Students are encouraged to contribute to the creation and maintenance of an orderly and supportive learning environment, where their efforts are acknowledged and celebrated within the school community. Any behavior that hinders learning within this environment is promptly addressed with appropriate action.


The management of JSIS ensures the establishment of a clear behavior policy Principals are tasked with implementing this policy and ensuring that all members of the school community understand the expectations and procedures for maintaining a positive learning environment. School staff members are responsible for adhering to and enforcing the policy and procedures within the school.


Student Responsibilities


1. Each student is accountable for respecting the rights of others within the school community.


2. It is the responsibility of every student to maintain personal hygiene and ensure neat and clean clothing.


3. Students are expected to respect the property of others, including school facilities, materials, and books.


4. Engaging in any form of fighting, disruptive behavior, or unlawful activity is strictly prohibited.


5. Students should adhere to punctuality by arriving promptly to school and classes.


6. Inappropriate behavior within the classroom environment is discouraged.


7. Students are encouraged to contribute to the preservation and protection of the school environment.


8. Possession, use, or transmission of alcohol, tobacco, or drugs is strictly forbidden.


9. Bullying, fighting, or arrogant behavior is addressed through various methods such as counselling, parental involvement, and assigning responsibilities.


10. Open communication channels are encouraged, allowing students to express concerns and seek guidance from school authorities.


Dealing with School/Class Absences


  • • Class teachers are directed to conduct random checks when a student is absent for two days or more.

  • • A leave note in the diary is mandatory for one or two days of absence, while an official leave letter is required for prolonged absences.

  • • Parents are contacted if a student takes extended leave without providing a letter.

  • • In cases of doubt, the driver/conductor is consulted to confirm the student's absence from the stop.

  • • If a student is caught bunking, their parents are contacted, and a warning/suspension letter is issued.

Anti-Bullying Policy

JSIS maintain a zero-tolerance stance against all forms of bullying, whether direct or indirect. Any observed or reported instances of bullying are thoroughly investigated, and appropriate measures are taken to prevent recurrence.


Responsibilities


  • • JSIS Management ensures the implementation of a clear anti-bullying policy across all schools.

  • • Principals oversee the implementation of the Anti-Bullying policy and develop unambiguous procedures for addressing bullying incidents.

  • • School staff are responsible for adhering to the policy and promptly addressing all reported or observed cases of bullying.

The Code: It's absolutely fine to report


Children are informed of whom they can approach in case of bullying incidents, including class teachers, trusted staff members, the Principal, or the Nurse. Children understand that all staff members take bullying incidents seriously.


How to Deal with an Incident of Bullying


Each bullying case is unique, but the following strategy provides a framework for addressing the issue:


Stage 1 – Do a thorough enquiry


  • • Interview each perpetrator individually to gather accurate information.

  • • Interview witnesses or bystanders, followed by the victim.

  • • Address the perpetrator directly, emphasizing the unacceptability of bullying and the need for change.

  • • Avoid accepting excuses or blaming the victim, instead encouraging empathy towards the victim.

  • • Consider having the perpetrator write down their account of the incident.

Stage 2 – Gentle counselling/ intervention


  • • Encourage the perpetrator to suggest ways to rectify the situation and support the victim.

  • • Facilitate discussions or activities that help the perpetrator understand the victim's perspective.

  • • Determine corrective actions, such as apologizing to the victim or making a behavioural contract.

  • • Schedule a follow-up meeting to monitor progress.

Stage 3 - Following Up action


  • • Conduct follow-up meetings with the perpetrator(s) and victim to assess progress.

  • • Continue monitoring the situation and arrange further meetings as needed.

Stage 4 – Further action


  • • Document all bullying incidents and actions taken in the Principal's logbook.

  • • Inform the Principal of serious incidents for appropriate intervention and parental involvement.

Supporting The Victim


  • • Provide a supportive environment where victims feel heard and valued.

  • • Pair victims with other children to encourage participation in activities and boost confidence.

Combat Bullying in The Longer Term


The school addresses bullying in the following ways:


  • • Raising awareness about bullying and discussing unacceptable behavior.

  • • Promoting pro-social behavior through regular group sessions or class teachings.

  • • Developing preventive strategies and support systems for victims.

  • • Regularly reviewing and updating the Anti-Bullying Policy.

  • • Providing training for staff to prevent bullying and foster a culture of mutual respect.
